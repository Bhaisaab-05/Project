Articles & Research

Scholarly articles and research papers on inquiry-based learning

A Meta-Analysis of the Effectiveness of Inquiry-Based Teaching

By M.A. Lazonder & R. Harmsen | Review of Educational Research | 2016

This meta-analysis examines how different types of guidance affect inquiry learning outcomes. The study analyzed results from 72 studies and found that inquiry learning is more effective when it includes specific types of guidance.

Teacher Professional Learning: Developing with the Inquiry Stance

By Marilyn Cochran-Smith & Susan L. Lytle | Teachers College Record | 2009

This influential article addresses how teachers can develop an "inquiry stance" that supports ongoing professional learning and effective implementation of inquiry approaches in the classroom.

Teacher Development Implementation Professional Learning
Read Full Article

Inquiry-Based Science Instruction—What Is It and Does It Matter?

By Andrew A. Zucker, et al. | Journal of Research in Science Teaching | 2010

This study examines the relationship between inquiry-based science instruction and student achievement and interest in science. The research demonstrates significant gains in critical thinking skills, scientific literacy, and positive attitudes toward science.
